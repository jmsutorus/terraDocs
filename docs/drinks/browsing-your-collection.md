# Browsing Your Collection

The Collection and Flights tabs give you two different ways to navigate your drinks archive.

## Home tab

The Home tab shows a **Featured Pour** — the highest-rated drink you've logged recently, or your highest-rated overall if nothing recent exists — and a **Recent Tastings** section showing the last three drinks you've logged. It's a snapshot, not a full view.

## Collection tab

The Collection tab shows your complete drinks archive in a searchable table.

**Search** — type any part of a name, producer, or type and the list filters instantly.

**Each row shows:**
- The drink photo (or a type-based placeholder)
- Name and producer
- Type
- Your overall rating
- The date you last logged a tasting
- A favorite heart badge if marked as a favorite

Tap any row to open the full drink detail page, where you'll see all your tasting logs, the full field set, and options to edit or log a new tasting.

## Flights tab

The Flights tab organizes your collection by drink type: Wine Flight, Beer Flight, Spirits Flight, Cocktail Flight, and so on. Each type shows its entries as a visual grid with drink photos, names, producers, and ratings.

Use the Flights tab when you want to see your wines separate from your beers, or when you're browsing a specific category to pick something to drink.

## Favorites

Drinks marked as favorites display a badge in the Collection tab. To see only your favorites quickly, scan the Collection tab for the favorite badge — there's no dedicated favorites filter, but favorites are visually distinct in the list.

## Common questions

**How do I find a drink I added a while ago?**
Use the search bar in the Collection tab. Search by name, producer name, or type. The search is real-time so a few letters is usually enough.

**Can I filter by status (e.g., see only my Stocked drinks)?**
Currently the Collection tab shows all drinks together. Use the Flights tab to browse by type, or use the search bar to type a producer or note that helps identify what you're looking for.

**What's the difference between the drink rating and the tasting log rating?**
The drink's overall rating is your stable view of how good it is. Tasting log ratings capture how a specific occasion went — a bottle that poured warm, a wine that was at peak, a cocktail that was made particularly well. Both are tracked separately.
