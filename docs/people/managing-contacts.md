# Managing Contacts

This page covers how to add people, what each field means, and how to keep profiles useful over time.

## Adding a person

Tap the **+** button on the People page. The form is split into sections:

**Section 1 — Identity**
- **Name** — required. Use whatever name you actually call this person.
- **Birthday** — month, day, and optionally year. If you don't know the year, toggle **Year Unknown** and only the month and day are stored. Terra will still surface their upcoming birthday without calculating age.
- **Profile photo** — upload a photo or paste a URL. Avatars show throughout the app wherever this person appears.

**Section 2 — Connection**
- **Relationship category** — Family, Friends, Work, or Other. This is required and used to filter and organize your directory.
- **Relationship type** — an optional custom label within that category. You can create your own types (e.g., "College Friend," "Book Club," "Therapist"). Types are reusable across multiple people.
- **Romantic partner** — toggle this on if this person is your current partner. This connects them to your Relationship section.

**Section 3 — Contact details**
- Email, phone, and address. All optional.

**Section 4 — Anniversaries & notes** *(visible when editing an existing person)*
- **Anniversary** — any recurring significant date you want to track (a wedding anniversary, the date you met, etc.)
- **Gift ideas** — a private, markdown-supported space to jot down things they'd like. Useful before birthdays and holidays.
- **Personal notes** — a full markdown editor for anything else: quirks you want to remember, things they've mentioned, context about how you know them. Supports Edit and Preview modes, zen mode for distraction-free writing, and font size control.

## Editing a person

Open any person's profile and tap **Edit**. All fields are editable at any time. Changes save when you confirm.

## Deleting a person

Use the delete option on a person's profile. Deleting a person removes their profile but does not remove tags on events, vacations, or other entries that referenced them.

## Relationship categories vs. relationship types

**Categories** are the four fixed buckets (Family, Friends, Work, Other). They determine how people are grouped in filtered views and what icons appear next to their names.

**Relationship types** are labels you create — more specific descriptions within a category. They're useful for:
- Keeping track of how people fit into your life ("College Friend" vs. "Childhood Friend" both in Friends)
- Sorting your directory in a way that makes sense to you
- Powering AI-generated summaries that reference who's in your life

You manage relationship types from within the People section. Add, edit, or delete them as your needs change.

## Tips

- **The gift ideas field is one of the most useful things you can keep here.** Whenever someone mentions something they want, or you notice something they'd love, add it immediately. Comes in very handy around gift-giving occasions.
- **Personal notes age well.** Even a few sentences about how you know someone or what matters to them can be surprisingly valuable to read back later.
- **Don't worry about being complete on day one.** Start with name and birthday. Add more as it becomes relevant.
