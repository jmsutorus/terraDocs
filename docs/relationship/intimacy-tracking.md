# Intimacy Tracking

The Intimacy tab is a private log for tracking your intimate life as a couple. Everything here is completely private — it is never visible to anyone other than you and cannot be published.

## Adding an entry

Tap the **+** button from the Intimacy tab.

**Fields:**
- **Date** — when it happened (defaults to today)
- **Time** — the time of day (optional)
- **Duration** — how long, in minutes (optional)
- **Satisfaction** — a 1–5 rating using a heart scale (optional)
- **Initiation** — who initiated: Me, Partner, or Mutual (optional)
- **Location** — Home, Away, or Other (optional)
- **Mood before** — how you felt going in: Excited, Neutral, Tired, Stressed, Relaxed, or Other (optional)
- **Mood after** — how you felt afterward: Satisfied, Neutral, Energized, Sleepy, Connected, or Other (optional)
- **Positions** — select from the positions library (optional)
- **Notes** — any private observations (optional)

None of the optional fields are required. A date alone is a valid entry.

## What the Intimacy tab shows

Each entry in the list displays the date, time, duration, satisfaction rating, mood badges (before and after), and any positions logged. A frequency indicator at the top shows how many entries you have in the last N days.

## Positions library

The Manage tab includes a positions library you can use when logging intimacy entries. It comes with a set of default positions (marked as System Defaults, which can't be deleted) and lets you add your own custom entries. Custom positions can be deleted at any time.

## Why track intimacy?

Over time the log surfaces patterns that are easy to miss in real life — frequency trends, how your mood before an encounter affects how you feel after, whether initiation balance has shifted. Some couples find the data useful for conversations about their relationship; others just find it grounding to have an honest record. Use it in whatever way is meaningful to you.

## Privacy reminder

The Intimacy tab displays a privacy banner as a persistent reminder that this data is visible only to you. It is never included in any published or shared content.
