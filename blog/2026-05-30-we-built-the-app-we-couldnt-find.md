It started with a familiar problem.

Open your phone and count the apps you use to track your life. There's the habit tracker. The mood journal. The workout log. The reading list. The trip planner. Maybe a separate one for movies and another for restaurants. Each app does its job well in isolation — but none of them talk to each other, and none of them tell you anything interesting about the whole picture.

That was the situation that started CairnHolm.

{/* truncate */}

## One Weekend, Six Apps, One Question

One weekend, sitting with six separate apps open, the question became unavoidable: *what if all of this lived in one place?*

Not in some sprawling productivity system that requires a PhD to configure. Not a minimalist two-feature app that runs out of room after a month. Something that could hold every dimension of a real life — the workouts and the moods, the concerts and the books, the parks and the relationships — and actually surface meaning from all of it together.

What started as a weekend project became a two-year obsession. The result is CairnHolm.

## What CairnHolm Is

CairnHolm is a personal command centre for habits, health, and everything worth remembering.

That's a deliberately broad description, because life doesn't split neatly into categories. You don't live your fitness separate from your mood, or your relationships separate from your travels. A tool that captures your life should reflect that.

At its core, CairnHolm gives you one place to:

- **Build habits** and watch them compound over weeks and months
- **Track your mood** and identify what's actually driving your best days
- **Log workouts** and let AI generate a training plan around your goals
- **Record the things you experience** — books, films, concerts, parks, restaurants, trips — so you never forget what moved you
- **Document the people** you share your life with and the milestones you reach together
- **Talk to an AI** that has context on all of the above

Twenty-plus areas of life, one dashboard.

## The Philosophy Behind It

Three ideas shaped every decision in building CairnHolm.

**Intentionality.** The best life isn't the busiest one — it's the most deliberate one. CairnHolm is built for people who want to live on purpose, not just on autopilot. When you can see your patterns clearly, you can make better choices about what to keep and what to change.

**Craftsmanship.** This is a tool you'll use every day, so it should feel good to use. The design is editorial and considered, not clinical. There are no dark patterns, no engagement traps, no algorithmic feeds pushing you toward more time in the app. Just a clean, thoughtful space for your data.

**Continuity.** The value of tracking compounds over time. A single mood log tells you almost nothing. A year of mood logs, cross-referenced with your exercise, sleep, and social activity, tells you everything. CairnHolm is built for the long game.

## One Builder, Real Software

There's something worth saying about how CairnHolm was made: by one person. Not a team of twenty, not a venture-backed startup with a roadmap designed around retention metrics. One developer, building the tool they actually wanted to use.

That means every feature exists because it was genuinely useful, not because a product manager needed to fill a quarterly milestone. It means the app respects your attention and your data. And it means the pace of development reflects real-world use — things get better when they need to be better, not when a sprint cycle says so.

Twenty-plus features. Two years of building. One dashboard.
